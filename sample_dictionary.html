TEI | TEI document338
P5 Guidelines: contains a single TEI-conformant document, containing a single TEI header, a single text, one or more members of the model.resourceLike class, or a combination of these. A series of TEI elements may be combined together to form a teiCorpus element. [more]
Project: For the Northwind Papers project, the <TEI> element represents a discrete object—such as a letter, a journal, or something else. A unique identifier should be added as an @xml:id attribute, so that the item's contents can be targeted directly within the project.
Example(s): 
   <TEI xml:id="l_18350102FMS_WHS1v3">  …  </TEI>
Attributes:
  • @xml:id
Contained by:
  • teiCorpus
May contain:
  • teiHeader
  • text

change795
P5 Guidelines: documents a change or set of changes made during the production of a source document, or during the revision of an electronic file. [more]
Project: <change> encodes milestones in the digital history of each digital file in the Northwind project, such as transcription, conversion to TEI, and publication. Smaller changes (such as edits to of a couple of lines) are recorded using commit notes in subversion. This element always appears within <listChange>, which is within <revisionDesc>.
Example(s): 
   <revisionDesc> 
      <listChange> 
         <change type="transcription" when="2014-05-01" who="npp:saz"/> 
         <change type="revision" when="2014-09-01" who="npp:mhr"/> 
      </listChange> 
   </revisionDesc>
Attributes:
  • @type
  • @when
  • @who
Contained by:
  • listChange
